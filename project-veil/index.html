THE VEIL

Laser Projection · Sacred Ceremony · Immersive

THE VEIL

A 10-minute immersive audio-visual ceremony performed in darkness. A single laser projector beams sacred geometry onto three hanging fabric veils — each veil representing a stage in the journey of the soul through the kingdoms of heaven. Symbols and designs are projected in sequence, all audio-reactive to a pre-composed devotional track. The light passes through each layer of fabric, splitting and diffracting as it travels deeper into the space.

Three veils. Three kingdoms. The first veil catches the sharpest geometry — clean lines, precise sacred forms. The second softens it — the symbols blur and merge as light passes through the weave. The third receives only ghosts — faint traces of what was projected, barely visible, felt more than seen. Participants stand between the veils. The ceremony moves through them.

THE THREE VEILS

I. The First Veil — The Material — Sharp sacred geometry projected directly onto the nearest fabric. Clean lines, precise symbols. The audience sees the designs at full intensity. The audio track begins — low drone, rising harmonic overtones. The laser draws each glyph in sequence, audio-reactive, pulsing with the composition.

II. The Second Veil — The Celestial — The beam passes through the first fabric and hits the second. Symbols soften, blur, merge with each other. The track builds — layered voices, resonant sub-bass. The geometry begins to overlap and interfere. What was precise becomes atmospheric.

III. The Third Veil — The Divine — Only traces reach the final fabric. Faint ghosts of the original symbols — barely visible, felt more than seen. The track resolves into a single sustained tone. The laser dims. Darkness returns. The ceremony ends in silence.

TECHNICAL

Single laser projector beaming through three suspended fabric veils. All visuals audio-reactive to a pre-composed 10-minute devotional track. Generative sacred glyph library — 25 symbols drawn as continuous laser paths. 10 minutes. 3 veils. No phones.

INTERACTIVE IDEA — MARFA INSTALLATION

Designed for a gallery space in Marfa, Texas — a 2m × 2m LED wall with a laser projector firing over the audience. Three jumbo illuminated dome buttons on a pedestal in the center of the room. Each button triggers a different veil — a different layer of sacred geometry, a different movement in the audio composition. The constraint of three buttons makes it feel like a public instrument. Each press is a small act of worship in a techno-religious order.
