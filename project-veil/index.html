THE VEIL

A 30-minute immersive audio-visual ceremony performed in complete darkness. Participants enter a blacked-out space and are surrounded by laser-projected sacred geometry — symbols drawn in real-time across every surface. The floor. The walls. The fog. The bodies of those standing beside you. There is no screen. There is no stage. The projection is omnidirectional — a 360-degree laser throw that maps the room itself as its canvas.

The symbols are generative, meaning no two ceremonies are identical. The system draws from a library of 25 sacred glyphs — geometric forms derived from ancient religious iconography, stripped to their structural essence and redrawn as single continuous laser paths. The audio is a purpose-built drone composition that evolves across seven movements. Sub-bass frequencies below the threshold of hearing create a physical sensation of pressure. The sound radiates from transducers embedded in the floor, so participants feel it rising through their feet.

THE SEVEN MOVEMENTS

I. Threshold — Total darkness. A single sub-bass tone rises through the floor. One point of light appears on the far wall. It holds for 90 seconds.

II. The Drawing — The dot begins to move. A single line traces the first sacred symbol — slow, deliberate. The glyph completes. Holds. Dissolves.

III. Multiplication — The laser splits. Two beams. Four. Eight. The fog machines engage — beams become visible as lines cutting through the air.

IV. The Procession — Symbols drift across the walls in slow procession. The audio introduces a pulse. Not a beat. Something biological.

V. Convergence — All symbols contract toward center. Beams cross through fog, creating a three-dimensional lattice of light above participants.

VI. Dissolution — The lattice breaks. Symbols fragment into raw geometry — lines, arcs, points of light scattering across every surface.

VII. The Clearing — One by one, the lights extinguish. The sound fades to a single tone. Then silence. Then darkness. 60 seconds of absolute nothing.

TECHNICAL

8x ILDA laser projectors (2W RGB) in 360° throw configuration, 30kpps scan rate per unit. Floor-mounted tactile transducers with sub-bass haptic range (18Hz–60Hz). Ambisonic spatial audio (3rd order). Cracked oil fog for volumetric beam visibility. Temperature controlled to 16°C. Minimum 12m × 12m × 4m ceiling. Maximum 60 participants per ceremony. 30 minutes. 7 movements. No intermission. No phones.
