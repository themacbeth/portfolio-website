HYDRO_VOID

An exploration of underwater architecture dissolved by time — structures that were never meant to exist beneath the surface, now claimed entirely by it.

The series began as a single still render: a brutalist hallway, half-submerged, light filtering through murky water in long green columns. Something about the quiet wrongness of it pulled me back.

C4D + Redshift · 2023–2024

Water became the main character. Not dramatic, churning water — but still, heavy, patient water. The kind that sits on top of things and waits.

Structures dissolving into something quieter than silence.

PROCESS

Built entirely in Cinema 4D with Redshift shaders tuned for caustic light scatter. Each environment was designed as a closed loop — no edges, no horizon — to create a sense of spatial entrapment that mirrors the emotional tone.

Volume fog and subsurface scattering were key to getting the water density right. Final grading was done in DaVinci Resolve to push the teal-to-black contrast.
